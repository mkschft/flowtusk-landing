Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know before your first flow.

Does this replace Zapier?

For Webflow, yes. Flowtusk connects directly to Webflow's native events without a Zapier account or subscription. If you're using Zapier purely to bridge Webflow and HubSpot, Flowtusk handles that and costs less. If you use Zapier across dozens of unrelated apps, Flowtusk sits alongside it, just handling the Webflow side.

What if the contact already exists in HubSpot?

Flowtusk checks by email address. If the contact exists, it updates their record. If not, it creates a new one. You won't end up with duplicate contacts.

Will this break my HubSpot attribution?

No. Flowtusk sends data via the HubSpot API, which preserves source, UTM parameters, and deal history. Your reporting stays intact. If you have specific attribution requirements, reach out before setting up.

Does Flowtusk work with my current Webflow plan?

Yes. All plans. Basic, CMS, Business, and Enterprise. You don't need a Webflow Enterprise contract to use it.

Can I test a flow before it goes live?

Yes. The Console node lets you debug a flow before activating it, so you can confirm data is being sent correctly before anything reaches HubSpot or Slack.

What Webflow events does Flowtusk support?

Form Submission, Site Publish, Page Created, Page Updated, Page Deleted, Ecomm New Order, Ecomm Order Changed, Ecomm Inventory Changed, User Account Added, Updated and Deleted, and all Collection Item events: Created, Changed, Deleted, Published, and Unpublished.

What happens if HubSpot or Slack is temporarily down?

Flowtusk queues the payload and retries. Your data doesn't disappear because a destination tool had a hiccup.
